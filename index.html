Taniti is a tropical island in the Pacific, come explore the varied terrain that includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

History

Taniti has a long and rich history. The islands were first settled by migrating Polynesians as early as 500BC.

They were later discovered by European explorers during the 16th century and eventually colonized by France.

Taniti is now an autonomous independant island nation. To learn more about our history, Please visit the Taniti History and Culture Museum.

Attractions

Sightseeing: Visit Taniti City to view beautiful native architecture and explore the nearby white, sandy beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay. Feeling more adventureous? Experience These exciting activities: boat or bus tours, rainforest hikes, chartered fishing tours, snorkeling, and zip-lining in the rainforest

Durring your stay, enjoy Local food that can be purchaced at Taniti's two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and oneconvenience store that is open 24 hours a day, or try one of the 11 restaurants on the island. Five serve local cuisine, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.

Entertainment: Need a Break from sightseeing? visit one of our local pubs(including a microbrewery!), enjoy the nightlife at our new dance club, see a movie at the Taniti Multiplex, Check out the many galleries of our thriving local art scene, or take the family to our bowling and arcade complex.

Local Transport: Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. every day. Private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis are available in Taniti City, and rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport. Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the power outlets in Taniti compatible with my electronics?

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

Are there any restrictions on the consumption of alcohol?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti, but is not strictly enforced.

Will I be able to get around without speaking the Tanitian Language?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

Is medical care availible on the Island?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Is Taniti safe?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, be sure to exercise caution.

Are amenities availible year round?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so please plan your visit accordingly.

What are my options for paying for goods and services in Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
